County Legislative Process

1

Find a Sponsor

Current Step
Contact your district supervisor or approach a committee chair. You'll need at least one supervisor to sponsor your proposal.
Interactive Supervisor Selector:

Draft Email to Supervisor

Dear Supervisor (District ),

I am a constituent in your district and would like to request your sponsorship for a [ordinance/resolution] regarding [brief description of issue].

The proposed legislation would:
- [Key provision 1]
- [Key provision 2]
- [Key provision 3]

I would appreciate the opportunity to discuss this with you further. Please let me know when we might meet to review this proposal.

Thank you,
[Your Name]

2

Draft with Corporation Counsel

The proposed legislation would:

  • [Key provision 1]
  • [Key provision 2]
  • [Key provision 3]

We would appreciate your assistance in drafting this legislation to ensure it meets all legal requirements and is properly formatted for consideration by the County Board.

Please let me know what additional information you need and when we might schedule a meeting to discuss this further.

3

Committee Review

Upcoming
Your proposal will be assigned to relevant committees based on subject matter. Public hearings may be held.
4

Board Vote

Upcoming
Full Board of Supervisors debates and votes. Majority (19 votes) needed to pass.
5

Executive Action

Upcoming
County Executive has 10 days to sign or veto. If vetoed, Board needs 25 votes (2/3) to override.

3

Introduction & Referral

Upcoming
Your alder introduces the item at a Common Council meeting. It gets referred to relevant committees.
4

Committee Review

Upcoming
Committees review, hold public hearings, and make recommendations to the full Council.
5

Council Vote

Upcoming
Full Common Council debates and votes. Majority (11 votes) needed to pass.
6

Mayoral Action

Upcoming
Mayor has 10 days to sign or veto. If vetoed, Council needs 14 votes (2/3) to override.
